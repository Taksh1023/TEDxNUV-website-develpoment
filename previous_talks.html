Meet Our Speaker of 2019


Viral Joshi

Profession: Wildlife recordist, Wildlife rescuer, Educator

Topic: Looking at birds through ears

Know about birds through the sounds they make His speech would be on ”Looking at the birds through your ears.” and from his experience in wild more than 6 years, he would be talking about the identification of the birds just by their calls. The bird enthusiast says he is comforted by recording the calls of birds and people have also dubbed him 'Acumen Acoustic'. The whole session would be an interactive session in which the sounds would be played and the audience would be just closing their eyes and guessing the common birds around them adding on to it the basic techniques of identification of the birds would be talked on.

Kimberly Shah

Topic: Your Health is a life long investment.

Fitness is a topic often ignored by most individuals and is often a cause of health problems and diseases. Kimberly Shah lays emphasis on the importance of fitness in one’s life. Dakxin Chhara


Topic: Theatre activism and Rehabilitation.

Dakxin Chhara comes from a de-notified tribe in Ahmedabad, in western India. For the tribe, it has always been difficult to get the treatment they deserve since they have always been regarded as ‘criminals’. Chhara shares his experiences on how he helped the tribe members get a basic income and permanent homes with the help of theater. Dakxin Chhara is an award winning filmmaker, playwright, director and an activist from the Chhara De-notified Tribes of Ahmedabad. He recently directed a critically acclaimed feature film "Sameer" which was released in September 2017. He is a recipient of Ford Foundation International Fellowship to study Graduate studies in ‘Theatre and Global Development’ at the University of Leeds, UK.
